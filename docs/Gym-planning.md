# Gym Programming Guide: How to Structure Your Training

## TL;DR — The 4 Programming Rules

1. **Training frequency**: Train each muscle **2× per week** (not 1×)
2. **Weekly volume**: **12–20 sets per muscle per week** (split however you want across days)
3. **Rep range**: **6–12 reps per set**, moderate weight, near-failure (1–2 reps in reserve)
4. **Cardio timing**: Keep running **separate from lifting sessions** (at least 6+ hours apart)

---

## Part 1: Training Frequency (2x Per Week Wins)

### The Principle

Train each muscle group **at least twice per week** for optimal hypertrophy. Each muscle should see two growth-stimulus windows per week.

### Why It Matters

- **1× per week**: One stimulus, then 6 days of rest before next
- **2× per week**: Two stimuli, spaced 3–4 days apart, more total growth signal

Research shows a **~15% advantage** for 2× per week frequency when total weekly volume is matched.

### Implementation Options

**Option A: Upper/Lower Split (4 days)** — chest/back/shoulders/arms day, legs day, repeat with variation

**Option B: Push/Pull/Legs (6 days)** — push day, pull day, legs day, repeat (3× per week per muscle)

**Option C: Body-Part Split (5+ days)** — only viable if each muscle is hit 2×/week (chest day, back day, then a re-hit later in the week)

For your 4-day schedule, **Upper/Lower split is the natural fit**.

## Part 2: Weekly Volume (The Volume Pyramid)

### The Principle

Total weekly sets per muscle drives hypertrophy:

| Weekly Sets/Muscle | Expected Outcome |
|--------------------|------------------|
| 6–10 sets | Minimal growth |
| 10–15 sets | Moderate growth |
| **12–20 sets** | **Optimal growth** |
| 20–30 sets | Diminishing returns |
| 30+ sets | Excessive (recovery suffers) |

### Volume Targets by Muscle

| Muscle | Min sets/week | Optimal sets/week |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Chest | 10 | 12–18 |
| Back | 10 | 14–20 |
| Shoulders | 8 | 12–16 |
| Quads | 10 | 12–18 |
| Hamstrings | 8 | 10–14 |
| Glutes | 8 | 10–16 |
| Biceps | 6 | 8–14 |
| Triceps | 6 | 8–14 |
| Calves | 4 | 6–10 |

**Total weekly sets**: roughly 80–130 sets across all muscles.

### Counting Volume

- Working sets to near-failure count
- Heavy warm-up sets count
- Light warm-ups (50% or less) do NOT count
- Drop sets / rest-pause: each piece counts as one set

## Part 3: Rep Ranges and Load Selection

### The Principle

Hypertrophy works across **light to heavy loads** if you train near failure. Strength (1RM) requires heavy loads.

### Three Useful Zones

| Rep range | Load | Best for | Notes |
|-----------|------|----------|-------|
| 3–6 reps | 80–90% 1RM | Strength + size | High joint stress, long rest |
| **6–12 reps** | **65–80% 1RM** | **Hypertrophy (best balance)** | The "sweet spot" |
| 12–20 reps | 50–65% 1RM | Hypertrophy + endurance | Easier on joints, accumulates fatigue |

### Recommended Default

**6–12 reps with 1–2 reps in reserve (RIR)** — meaning, stop the set when you have 1–2 reps left in the tank, not all-out failure on every set.

### When to Push to Failure

- Last set of an isolation exercise (curls, lateral raises, leg extensions)
- Never on heavy compound lifts (bench, squat, deadlift) — fatigue cost is too high

## Part 4: Concurrent Training (Cardio + Lifting)

### The Principle

Same-session cardio + lifting can blunt muscle gains via **molecular interference** (mTOR signaling pathway disruption).

### Practical Rules

| Strategy | Interference? |
|----------|---------------|
| Cardio AFTER lifting (same session) | Mild interference |
| Cardio BEFORE lifting (same session) | Strong interference |
| Cardio on separate day | None |
| Cardio 6+ hours apart from lifting | Minimal |
| Low-intensity walking on lifting day | None |

### Optimal Setup

- **Lifting days**: lifting only
- **Running days**: running only, ideally on a separate day
- **Walking**: fine to do daily, no interference
- **HIIT or hard cardio**: keep ≥24 hrs from heavy leg day

## Part 6: Progressive Overload

### The Goal

Add **reps, sets, or weight over time**. This is the engine of growth.

### The Double-Progression Method

For each exercise, pick a rep range (e.g., 6–10 reps).

1. Start with a weight you can do for **6 reps** with 1–2 RIR
2. Each session, try to add **1 rep**
3. When you hit **10 reps** for all your sets, increase weight by ~5%
4. Drop back to 6 reps and repeat the climb

### Example

- Week 1: Bench 80 kg × 6 reps × 4 sets
- Week 2: Bench 80 kg × 7 reps × 4 sets
- Week 3: Bench 80 kg × 8 reps × 4 sets
- Week 4: Bench 80 kg × 10 reps × 4 sets (top of range)
- Week 5: Bench 85 kg × 6 reps × 4 sets (jump up)

### Deload Every 4–8 Weeks

- One week of 50–60% volume
- Same weights, fewer sets, more reps in reserve
- Allows joints, CNS, and tendons to recover

## Part 7: Exercise Selection Hierarchy

### Tier 1 — Big Compound Lifts

Do these first when fresh. Use 6–8 rep range.

- Squat (back or front)
- Deadlift (conventional or Romanian)
- Bench press (flat or incline)
- Overhead press
- Barbell row
- Pull-up / chin-up

### Tier 2 — Secondary Compound

Use 8–12 rep range, after Tier 1.

- Dumbbell bench
- Dumbbell row
- Lat pulldown
- Bulgarian split squat
- Leg press
- Dumbbell shoulder press

### Tier 3 — Isolation

Use 10–15 rep range, end of session.

- Curls (any variation)
- Triceps pushdowns
- Lateral raises
- Face pulls
- Leg extensions / leg curls
- Calf raises

### Selection Rules

- 60–70% of volume from Tier 1 + Tier 2 compound lifts
- 30–40% from Tier 3 isolation
- Pick exercises that don't aggravate joints (more important than picking "the best" exercise)

## Part 8: Rest Times Between Sets

| Rep range | Rest time | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| 1–5 reps (heavy) | 3–5 min | Full ATP/CP recovery for next heavy set |
| 6–10 reps | 2–3 min | Balance between recovery and density |
| 10–15 reps | 90 sec – 2 min | Maintain quality without rushing |
| 15+ reps (isolation) | 60–90 sec | Metabolic stress benefits |

**Rule of thumb**: rest until your breathing has settled and you feel ready to perform the next set with quality. Do not rush.

## Part 9: Programming for Specific Goals

### Goal: Hypertrophy (most people)

- Volume: 12–20 sets per muscle per week
- Reps: 6–12 (mostly), some 12–20
- Frequency: 2× per week per muscle
- Failure: 1–2 RIR most sets
- Progression: double-progression

### Goal: Strength (1RM PRs)

- Volume: 8–12 sets per muscle per week
- Reps: 1–6 mostly
- Frequency: 2–3× per week (compound lifts)
- Failure: 2–3 RIR (avoid grinding reps)
- Progression: linear or block periodization

### Goal: Calisthenics Skills (handstand, muscle-up, etc.)

- Skill practice: 5–10 min daily, fresh
- Strength work: pull-up volume, dip volume, core stability
- Mobility: shoulder, wrist, thoracic
- Hypertrophy is supportive, not the priority — keep at 10–14 sets per muscle/week

### Goal: Fat Loss + Muscle Preservation

- Volume: maintain 12–16 sets per muscle/week (don't reduce)
- Reps: 6–12 mostly (heavier loads protect strength)
- Frequency: 2× per week
- Failure: 1–2 RIR (deeper fatigue is harder to recover from in a deficit)
- Cardio: 1–3 sessions per week, separated from lifting
- Protein: 2.0–2.4 g/kg/day (see encyklopedia)

## Part 10: Common Programming Mistakes

| Mistake | Why it hurts | Fix |
|---------|------------|-----|
| Training each muscle once a week | Loses ~15% of growth potential | Move to 2× per week |
| Doing 30+ sets per muscle | Recovery suffers, no extra growth | Cap at 20 sets |
| Going to failure on every set | CNS fatigue, slower progression | Use 1–2 RIR most sets |
| Same routine for years | No progressive overload | Track loads, push reps/weight every week |
| Cardio before lifting | Interference effect | Lift first, or separate days |
| No deload | Joint pain, plateau | Deload every 4–8 weeks |
| Skipping warm-ups | Injury risk | 5–10 min general + light specific sets |
